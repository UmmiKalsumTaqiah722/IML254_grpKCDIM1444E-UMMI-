EXO

Background

Exo is a well-known boy band from South Korea consisting of 9 members, created by SM Entertainment. The members of the current lineup are Sehun, Baekhyun, Chanyeol, Xiumin, Suho, Lay, Chen, D.O., and Kai. With the release of their little album, Mama, on April 12, 2012, they made their official debut. EXO gained popularity after the release of their first studio album, XOXO, and became one of the most popular artists worldwide as well as in South Korea. They won many awards both locally and abroad, broke record after record, and secured huge brand deals.

When EXO originally debuted with 12 members, they were divided into two sub-groups:

1. EXO-K unit
This unit was focused on South Korean content; the members included Suho, Baekhyun, Chanyeol, Kai, and D.O.

2. EXO-M unit
Meanwhile, this unit was focused on China. had Xiumin, Lay, Chen, and former members Kris, Luhan, and Tao.

However, EXO started actively promoting as a full 9 member group in 2014 after the old members left.
