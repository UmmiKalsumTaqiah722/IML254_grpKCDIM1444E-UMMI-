SM ENTERTAINMENT

Lee Soo-man created the South Korean global entertainment company SM Entertainment in 1995. It is one of the biggest entertainment firms in South Korea and has helped numerous K-pop singers build their careers and become well-known worldwide, gaining an enormous fan following. The company performs various functions, such as music publishing, event management, music production, talent agency, record label, and concert production. SM Entertainment gained popularity for leading the global K-pop trend and the musical aspect of "Hallyu," also referred to as the "Korean Wave," with early international successes including BoA, S.E.S., and H.O.T.

The label has represented Hyun Jin-Young, H.O.T., S.E.S., Shinhwa, Fly to the Sky, The Grace, f(x), and Henry Lau in the past. Presently, it represents K-pop artists such as Kangta, BoA, TVXQ, Super Junior, Girls' Generation, J-Min, Shinee, Zhou Mi, Exo, Red Velvet, NCT, WayV, SuperM, Aespa, Got the Beat, and Riize. Along with managing actors like Yoo Ho-jeong, Cho Jun-young, and Lee Jae-ryong, it has formerly represented Go Ara and Lee Yeon-hee. In Japan, SM co-publishes Avex Trax releases for Johnny's Entertainment artists such as Arashi and Kat-Tun, in addition to Ayumi Hamasaki, Namie Amuro, and Koda Kumi.

YG ENTERTAINMENT

Yang Hyun-suk, a former member of Seo Taiji and Boys, formed YG Entertainment in 1996. With early hits like Jinusean and 1TYM, the company swiftly became a major force in the K-pop scene. With the release of Big Bang in 2006 and 2NE1 in 2009, the company achieved unimaginable success and established its image as a producer of creative and marketable talents. 2010 saw YG go public, and 2012 saw Psy's worldwide smash "Gangnam Style" gain previously unheard-of levels of attention from around the world. After Blackpink's 2016 debut, YG's global popularity grew even more, positioning them as one of the most well-known girl groups globally. YG succeeded despite the scandals that resulted in Yang Hyun-suk's departure in 2019, growing the number of artists and collaborating with foreign countries. Important musicians like Blackpink, Psy, Big Bang, and 2NE1 have contributed significantly to YG's success. The company's innovations, such as YG Family Concerts, different sub-labels, and clever international alliances, have secured its position as a major player in the entertainment sector, consistently influencing contemporary K-pop trends and expanding its influence internationally.

PLEDIS ENTERTAINMENT

Pledis Entertainment was founded in 2007 by Sung Soo Han. Son Dam-bi was the first artist to debut under Pledis and was dubbed ‘female Rain’ by media outlets. In its beginning stages, the agency did not have management premises, only a practice room. In January 2009, Pledis debuted its first girl group, After School, and a sub-unit of After School called Orange Caramel that was formed in 2010. In March 2012, Pledis debuted their first boy band, NU’EST. After that, in May 2015, the thirteen-member boy group Seventeen debuted after training under the company for several years.

From 2020 until present, Pledis Entertainment officially announced the acquisition of the majority shareholdership of Hybe Corporation, formerly Big Hit Entertainment, on May 25, 2020. It was also announced that Pledis Entertainment will receive financial and expert support from Hype Labels while still operating as an independent label. On March 22, 2021, Pledis Entertainment, as well as the other labels part of Hybe Labels, moved to its new headquarters in the Yongsan Trade Centre, the new headquarters of Hybe Corporation. On January 22, 2024, Pledis Entertainment CEO and Producer Han Sung-soo spearheaded the production for TWS’ debut extended play, Sparkling Blue.

BIGHIT ENTERTAINMENT

BigHit Entertainment, now HYBE Corporation, is a South Korean entertainment company renowned for its groundbreaking approach to the music industry. Founded in 2005 by Bang Si-hyuk, it gained international acclaim with the meteoric rise of BTS in 2013, propelling K-pop to new heights. BigHit's success with BTS led to the expansion of its roster with the addition of other talented acts, such as TXT (Tomorrow X Together), enhancing the company's reputation as a powerhouse in the K-pop industry. In addition to music production and artist management, BigHit Entertainment has diversified its business portfolio to include ventures in content production, merchandise, and technology. Beyond music, HYBE expanded its portfolio to include artist management, content production, and technology ventures. The company's rebranding in 2021 as HYBE Corporation reflects its evolution into a global entertainment powerhouse under Bang Si-hyuk's leadership, continually setting new standards and reshaping the cultural landscape.
